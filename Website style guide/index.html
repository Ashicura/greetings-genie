Fonts

Serif

Often used for the main body of text, serif fonts are more stylized than sans serif fonts, serif describes the samller details on letters. Some good examples are:

Bodoni Mai

Cormorant

Old Standard TT

Rokkitt

Suranna

Sans Serif

Often used for headings or to make things stand out, sans serif fonts are simpler than serif. San serif can be literaly translated to with out serif. Some examples are:

Acme

Anton

Electrolize

Krona One

Noto Sans TC


Text Styles

H1: Main page heading

  • Font-weight: 700 (bold)
  • Font sixe: 26px
  • Font-Family: Nunito Sans

H2: Subheading

  • Font-weight: 500
  • Font-size: 18px
  • Font-Family: Poppins

P: paragraph

  • Font-weight: 400 (regular)
  • Font-size: 14px
  • Font-Family: Poppins
